# Attribution

This repository applies established Knowledge-Based Product Development (KBPD)
principles to coding-agent workflows. It does not claim to invent KBPD,
K-Briefs, set-based learning, or Lean Product and Process Development.

## Primary Inspiration

The primary inspiration for this starter is:

- *Ready, Set, Dominate: Implement Toyota's Set-Based Learning for Developing
  Products and Nobody Can Catch You* by Michael Kennedy, Kent Harmon, and Ed
  Minnock, Oaklea Press, 2008.

The repository also draws on the broader KBPD and Lean Product and Process
Development tradition associated with Allen C. Ward, including work published
through the Lean Enterprise Institute and later development with Durward K.
Sobek II.

Useful bibliographic references:

- Google Books entry for *Ready, Set, Dominate*:
  https://books.google.com/books?id=CubtAAAAMAAJ
- Lean Enterprise Institute page for *Lean Product and Process Development*:
  https://www.lean.org/store/book/lean-product-and-process-development-2nd-edition/

## What This Repository Contributes

This repository contributes a practical Git- and agent-oriented implementation:

- a `.kbriefs/` directory structure;
- concise K-Brief templates;
- always-on `AGENTS.md` operating rules;
- portable Agent Skills that operate on K-Briefs;
- lightweight structural validation;
- examples showing how evidence becomes reusable engineering knowledge.
